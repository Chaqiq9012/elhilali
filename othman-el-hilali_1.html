The Author

Othman
El Hilali

Born in the coastal light of Casablanca, Othman El Hilali grew up between two languages, two worlds, and two versions of every story. That duality became the soil from which his writing grew — rich, contradictory, alive.

With over twelve published works, his books have crossed borders, been translated into seven languages, and found their way into the hands of more than 340,000 readers worldwide. His writing moves between literary fiction, personal essay, and poetry — always searching for the truest thing a sentence can hold.

New Release

The Echoes We Leave
Behind

What remains after we are gone? Not the grand gestures — but the small ones. The coffee made without asking. The window left open. The name spoken differently. This novel follows three strangers across three continents, each carrying the invisible weight of someone else's unfinished story. A book about inheritance, grief, and the strange grace of being remembered imperfectly.
